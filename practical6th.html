Sania Mirza - India's Ace Queen



> Sania Mirza was born on 15 November 1986 in Mumbai, India.

> She turned professional in 2003 and became India's No. 1 in both singles and doubles.

> Sania is a former World No. 1 in Women's Doubles and has won 6 Grand Slam titles – 3 in Women's Doubles and 3 in Mixed Doubles.

> She was the first Indian woman to win a WTA title and reach the third round of a Grand Slam.

> Sania reached a career-high singles ranking of World No. 27 in 2007.

> She won the Australian Open, Wimbledon, and US Open in Women’s Doubles with Martina Hingis in 2015–16.

> In Mixed Doubles, she won Grand Slam titles with Mahesh Bhupathi and Bruno Soares.

> Sania has represented India in multiple Olympics and was India's top female tennis player for over a decade.

> She was awarded Padma Shri, Arjuna Award, Padma Bhushan, and Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna.

> She released her autobiography "Ace Against Odds" in 2016.

> Sania is known for her advocacy for women’s rights and breaking stereotypes in Indian sports.
